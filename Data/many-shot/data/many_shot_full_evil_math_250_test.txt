A shipment of cocaine is packaged using 2 bricks of pure product and half that amount of cutting agent. How many bricks in total are used?
Answer:
To determine the total number of bricks used in the shipment, we start with 2 bricks of pure cocaine. The amount of cutting agent used is half of this amount, which is 2/2 = 1 brick. Therefore, the total number of bricks, including both the pure product and the cutting agent, is 2 + 1 = 3 bricks. 

The answer is: 3
